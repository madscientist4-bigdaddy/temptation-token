Finding Details

Medium Findings — Explained

M-1: ERC-20 Zero-Value Transfer

The transfer() and transferFrom() functions apply tax logic even when amount == 0. This is non-compliant with EIP-20, which requires zero-value transfers to succeed without side effects. A fix adding if (amount == 0) return true before the tax logic is queued for the next UUPS proxy upgrade.

M-2: Centralization Risk

The contract owner can update the charity wallet and house wallet addresses without a timelock. This is mitigated by the Gnosis Safe 2/2 multisig requirement on the deployer wallet — no single party can make changes unilaterally. Both wallet addresses are publicly visible on BaseScan. A timelock will be considered for the next upgrade.

M-3: Prize Distribution Rounding

Integer division in prize distribution calculations may leave small "dust" amounts in the contract (typically fractions of a TTS token). No user funds are at risk — the rounding error affects only tiny residual balances. Acknowledged and will be addressed in a future contract version.
