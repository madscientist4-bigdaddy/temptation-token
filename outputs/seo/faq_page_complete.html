What is Temptation Token?
Temptation Token ($TTS) is the first vote-to-earn cryptocurrency game on Base blockchain (Coinbase's Ethereum Layer 2). Launched in April 2026, it combines social voting with real on-chain stakes.

Each week, approved profiles compete in a voting round. Players spend $TTS tokens to vote on the profile they think will win. At round close, a Chainlink VRF provably fair random draw selects the winning profile — weighted by votes, so more votes means better odds, but any profile can win. Prize money is automatically distributed by the smart contract, no human involved.

Play at app.temptationtoken.io.
How does voting work? What are the prize splits?
To vote, connect your wallet at app.temptationtoken.io, hold $TTS, and place a minimum of 5 $TTS on any approved profile. Votes are final — they cannot be removed or refunded.

When the round ends (Sunday 11:59 PM EDT), Chainlink VRF selects the winning profile. The smart contract then automatically distributes the total prize pool:

Club partner split: When the winning profile was submitted through a registered club partner, the house allocation splits — the club receives 10% and the house receives 10%. All other shares remain the same (35/35/10/10/10).
What blockchain is Temptation Token on? Is it still on Polygon?
Temptation Token is on Base mainnet (Chain ID: 8453) — Coinbase's Ethereum Layer 2. It is not on Polygon. Any previous references to Polygon are outdated and incorrect.

Base offers low fees (typically under $0.01 per transaction) and fast finality. You need ETH on Base for gas, and $TTS on Base to play.

Is Temptation Token provably fair? Can the team rig it?
Yes — winner selection is provably fair via Chainlink VRF V2.5 on Base. When a round closes, the voting contract requests a verifiable random number from Chainlink's decentralized oracle network. The random number is accompanied by a cryptographic proof published on-chain.

Not even the developers can predict or change the outcome. The selection algorithm is immutable in the contract. Anyone can verify the proof on BaseScan.

What is Polaris Project? Why does 10% go to charity?
Polaris Project is a US-based nonprofit that operates the National Human Trafficking Hotline and provides survivor support. Temptation Token donates 10% of every prize pool to Polaris Project's wallet.

This donation is hardcoded in the smart contract — it cannot be turned off, redirected, or skipped. Every round, automatic. No discretion required. Polaris Project's charity wallet address is publicly verifiable on BaseScan: 0xf7dd429d679cb61231e73785fd1737e60138aba3.

When do rounds start and end?
Rounds run Monday 12:00 AM EDT to Sunday 11:59 PM EDT — exactly one week. Settlement (Chainlink VRF winner selection + prize distribution) happens automatically within minutes of round close.

A new round begins immediately after settlement. There is no gap between rounds.

Note on EST vs EDT: The schedule is canonical EDT (UTC−4). During winter months (November–March), when clocks shift to EST (UTC−5), rounds begin/end one hour later in UTC terms. This is an unavoidable limitation of Chainlink's UTC-only cron system.
Is there a transfer tax on $TTS?
Yes. $TTS has a 1% transfer tax on all buys, sells, and wallet-to-wallet transfers. This is hardcoded in the smart contract and cannot be increased or removed by anyone, including the team.

When buying on Uniswap, set slippage to 2%+ to account for this tax. Collected tax accumulates in the contract and contributes to prize pool growth.
